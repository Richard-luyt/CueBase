1.      In The Laugh of the Medusa, Cixous is calling women to write for themselves. She considers
writing as a method of “returning to body”. Hence escaping from the history created by
phallocentrism. Cixous believes that women should break out the snare of silence, and take the
challenge of speaking by writing. Cixous utilized rhetoric to describe writing as a returning to body
and weapons of fighting against phallocentrism
2.      Cixous critiques the classic conceptions of bisexuality as a self-effacing merger that tries to
erase sexual difference to avoid the fear of sectility. Instead, Cixous presented a new concept of
bisexuality (other bisexuality) based on the multiplication of the effects of the inscription of desire. It
is about not being neuter, but about the non-exclusion of either sex.
3.      From the video, there are several reasons that women are excluded from rhetoric. Rhetoric
emerges from history, and the fact is that women have been excluded from politics and laws in the
western traditions. Also, women’s speech is often associated with gossips and empty speech, which
reflects a deep sexist in the rhetoric tradition. And finally, making an orator is synonymous with
making a man and elite.
4.      Cixous challenges the authority of history by critique and creation, breaking the code that
negates her, and creating new styles, structures, forms of argument and a new identity of women.
